Written by Martin Luenendonk
Written by Martin Luenendonk

With more and more shoppers going online to purchase just about everything because of the times we are in, starting an eCommerce business is a great way to create a second income from the comfort of your laptop and home.

Aside from generating additional revenue, having an e-commerce store or business allows you to monetize your online presence fairly fast.

Own a potentially profitable blog or looking to start one? Sell some e-books or courses linked to your blog’s content via your store.I

Have a big social media following (even if it’s only on Instagram)? Direct them to your store to purchase your favorite products.

The retail e-commerce market is expected to hit $4.13 trillion dollars in 2020. That coupled with global economic instability make it the perfect time to start an ecommerce store.

So how do you start an ecommerce store? By first choosing an e-commerce platform.

Considering that there are over 400 options, it can be confusing!

In this guide we’ll review the best e-commerce platforms based on

  • ease of inventory management (adding new products and managing stock).
  • availability of discounts, coupons and any feature that helps convert sales.

By the end of this article, you’ll be able to confidently choose an e-commerce platform for your online store that suits your needs and will help you succeed in your online business.

From hundreds of e-commerce platforms, we have narrowed it down to the options below. Some of them are quite popular, but there are an equal number of less popular options that provide excellent services plus are both user and budget-friendly.

1. Shopify.

Most Trusted e-commerce platform for dropshipping and Print-On-Demand Stores. Plans start at $29/month.

This e-commerce giant has built a brand so strong that when you mention e-commerce or an online store, most people automatically think Shopify.

Powering over one million online stores globally, Shopify is arguably one of the fastest-growing service-based software companies today.

What makes Shopify so popular is how easy it is for anyone to start a store. It comes with a drag-and-drop storefront builder and access to over 3,000 apps in the Shopify store.

You can technically start your online store, complete with inventory in under 20 minutes. This is partly possible because Shopify allows you to find and import products from sites like Oberlo and AliExpress directly into your store and have them drop-shipped. 

Aside from lots of app integrations and easy set-up, you also get over 32 payment gateway options that work in all parts of the globe.

Shopify makes it easy to sell on social media, in person using Shopify POS or in any online marketplace.

When it comes to payments, Shopify has their own service called Shopify payments. So if you’re uncomfortable with any of the third party payment gateways listed on their platform, simply use Shopify payments to handle all your payments

Shopify payments is fully-PCI compliant and comes with secure 3D checkout. They accept all major credit and debit cards as well.

Some other features include

  • Easy drag-and-drop store builder with 70+ themes to choose from
  • Custom domains available or simply connect your store to an existing domain
  • Simple easy to use dashboard for managing your store and checking reports
  • Embed your products easily anywhere be it WordPress, Squarespace or any other website
  • Customizable Buy Buttons that can fit wherever you post your products
  • Direct sales through Instagram, Facebook Messenger or your Facebook shop.
  • Sync your store directly with Amazon and eBay

You’ll have three plans to choose from to get started.

Basic Shopify – starting from $29/month which covers the basics of owning and running an online store.

Shopify – $79/month which allows you up to 5 retail store locations, 5 staff accounts and USPS Priority mail pricing.

Advanced Shopify – starting from $299/month and comes with 15 staff accounts, 74% shipping discounts from major carriers, allows you to add third-party carriers and show your own shipping rates.

All plans come with unlimited products, an e-commerce website and blog, fraud analysis, shipping discounts, POS and abandoned cart recovery.

Already have a website? You can try Shopify Lite which lets you add products to your website/blog and accept credit card payments for only $9 USD per month.

What Are Ecommerce Platforms?

Ecommerce platforms are web-based software tools or applications that allow you to create and manage an online store to sell products or services. They offer all the necessary tools and functionality to manage and track online sales transactions.

Assuming you wanted to sell your products or business services online a few years ago, you’d have needed a lot of resources and funds.

First, you would’ve had to find your own hosting, be it paying for your own server or subscribing to a high tier web hosting service.

Next you’d have hired developers to code and create a software that allows you to list products/services, manage inventory and customers, track transactions, and payments. They’d have to constantly update the software and fix bugs too.

Today because of Service-as-a-Software (SaaS) technology, tech companies have been able to consolidate these together as a service and have made them available to aspiring entrepreneurs as software. Hence the birth of e-commerce platforms.

For free or for a monthly subscription, you can sign up to an e-commerce platform, and easily create a store from scratch, complete with inventory and payment processors.

Most e-commerce platforms handle the basics of creating and running an online store including design, inventory, customer management, shipping and returns as well as taxes.

They also offer integration with third-party apps from business-related service providers, social media platforms and marketplaces like Amazon and eBay.

Thanks to SaaS technology, e-commerce platforms are now readily available and easily accessible to anyone looking to sell online. There’s enough variety to support businesses of different sizes, needs and different experience levels.

  • What’s your level of tech-knowledge and web development skills? Are you willing to invest a little extra time in improving them?
  • Do you want to host your ecommerce site yourself or on the platform’s server?

What Are the Best Ecommerce Platforms for Dropshipping?

Dropshipping has become a popular ecommerce model in recent times. It’s a kind of retail fulfillment method where you act as the storefront and face of a wholesaler, manufacturer or even another retailer.

So you display and market the manufacturer’s goods or products, without keeping inventory. When customers order products, you simply pass the order on to the manufacturer to ship directly to the customer, while making a profit.

Lots of new online stores choose to build their business exclusively on dropshipping. It saves you from having to invest upfront in inventory, finding storage and cost of packaging.

An ecommerce platform designed to support dropshipping should first of all, give you ready access to a marketplace of wholesalers or manufacturers like AliExpress or Oberlo.

While you could technically just get the orders and manually go to any online store to place the order on your customer’s behalf, that process is tedious and lengthy. Apart from that, the last thing you want is to find out a customer has ordered something out of stock.

Dropshipping platforms help you avoid all the hassle by syncing stock and inventory levels so you can sell the items as though you have the stock physically. They also give you room to brand, market and promote these products as your own.

A good drop shipping ecommerce service should allow you to easily add wholesalers’ products to your store, automate shipping, sync inventory levels. You should also have flexibility to offer discounts and promotions as though the products are yours.

Aside from what we’ve mentioned already, there are other obvious features to look out for.

The platform should be user-friendly especially if you have a team with differing levels of tech knowledge.

  • It should support a dropshipping plugin or have its own native dropshipping plugin
  • It should have responsive and round-the-clock customer support.
  • It should make managing your store seamless and support scalability.

The number one benefit of using an ecommerce platform is the ability to be open for business at all times. The internet runs 24/7, meaning potential customers should be able to make purchases any day, anytime. 

Ecommerce platforms give online business owners a simple way to always be accessible to customers no matter their geographical location or time zone. Same applies to owners of physical stores.

By creating an online store, you can extend your business hours without necessarily having to keep your physical store open round the clock. 

2. Get started no matter your budget (or lack of one)

One of the biggest roadblocks to starting any kind of business is capital. Physical store locations come with lots of start-up costs including rent, labour, furnishing etc. An ecommerce platform removes all those expenses. 

Again, before ecommerce platforms became popular, the costs involved in building an online store were staggering. You would have needed a dedicated hosting,hired a developer to code, customize and build the front end of your store as well to maintain it and fix any bugs.

Now, all you need is a domain name, good web hosting and a source of traffic. Most ecommerce platforms provide hosting for your website plus allow unlimited products and bandwidth. Some also offer a free domain name for a year. Most are free to sign-up or prices as low as $20/month.

Ecommerce platforms can  give you all these sometimes for less than the price of dinner at a regular restaurant. Hence the barriers to starting a business online are much lower. The variety of free and paid options make it more easy and appealing to start-ups.

3. Manage your business from anywhere

Owning an online business appeals to many because of freedom and flexibility of working from anywhere, on any device. Using an ecommerce platform gives you this same freedom and flexibility. Log in from anywhere on any device that’s got an internet connection.

To make it even better, a number of platforms have the mobile app version from which you can manage your store on the go. Track inventory, shipments and customer purchases all from your phone. No need to spend time commuting to a physical store location just to conduct business.

Again thanks to SaaS technology, you won’t need to get your own hardware/software in-house just to run your store. Using an ecommerce platform eliminates this hassle.

4. Start your business without owning inventory

Want to start a business without the upfront investment in  physical goods and renting warehouses? An ecommerce platform that supports dropshipping will simplify the entire process in minutes.

No need to look for your own vendors or products because most platforms that support dropshipping come with built -in plugins that give you instant access to millions of wholesale vendors and dropship manufacturers in every niche e. g. Oberlo and Modalyst.

The apps also let you automatically import the manufacturers images, product descriptions and sync their inventory levels in your store so you don’t have to worry about keeping up tabs with product levels.

Apart from dropshipping, ecommerce platforms also support digital product and subscription based sales which eliminates the  Stress of handling shipping, order fulfilment and returns.

5. Reduce payroll and labour costs

A traditional store will need anywhere from 2 to 10 employees to stay operational, not including independent contractors. These all inflate the cost of starting and running a small business. With the ecommerce business model, you’ll see you don’t need as many hands to start.

Using an ecommerce platform also allows you to streamline your entire workflow. keep your pulse on your store’s numbers and taxes with  apps and extensions that do the heavy lifting for you.

With a dropshipping based store you even cut costs further since the manufacturer handles shipping and order fulfilment for you. Technically you can run a dropshipping store by yourself.

6. Quickly find a ready market online

Ecommerce platforms make it easier to find customers who are looking to buy what you sell. They let you use cost-effective ways like social media, organic search, and Google Adwords to reach potential customers.

If you don’t already have an existing online presence, some platforms boost your page rank in search engines helping put your website in front of more eyeballs e.g. 3dcart.

Others allow you to integrate your store with facebook and instagram, allowing you to tap into your social media following. You’ll also get integrations marketplaces like Amazon and eBay which are great for finding new customers, boosting revenue.

Still on marketing, you also have the option to boost sales conversion by integrating email marketing, discounts, free shipping and gift cards.

7. Make data-driven business decisions

Most ecommerce platforms provide in-depth sales and marketing analytics reports to help you understand how your store is doing.

Track which sales channels are bringing the most traffic and how your ad campaigns are doing. Get to know which products are customer favorites, which promotions work best and even your average conversion  rates and visitor traffic.

8. Manage your customers in one place

The number one way to build a growing and consistently profitable store is through repeat customers. A five percent increase in customer retention can lead to an increase in profits of between 25 and 95 percent (SmallBiz Trends). Repeat customers spend 33 percent more than new customers. 

Stats like this only go to prove how necessary it is to manage and maintain your existing customer base. With a traditional business, this process tends to be tedious.

An ecommerce platform integrates customer management into the platform making it easy to maintain and grow a customer base.

Types of eCommerce Platforms

By now, you’re familiar with popular ecommerce platforms and know the basic functionality that such platforms offer. Apart from features, you can further classify ecommerce platforms based on their access and how they operate.

There are three main kinds of ecommerce platforms. Each one comes with its unique perks and downsides. The better you know your ecommerce business goals, the easier it will be to pick a platform that suits your needs. 

1. Self-hosted (Open-source)

As the name implies, self- hosted ecommerce platforms are platforms that give you flexibility when it comes to hosting. You can use the platforms with your existing web hosting service. 

Most self-hosted platforms are also open-source meaning it’s source code is readily available for users and developers to access and modify. It also means bugs and any necessary fixes are done quickly because developers are constantly working to improve its performance.

For you as an end user, an open-source ecommerce platform allows full customization. You can choose how each part of your store looks, responds and behaves. Be it colors, fonts, forms, check-out pages etc with a little coding knowledge.

Open-source platforms often have their own marketplace of extensions and apps which allow you to add more functionality to your website e.g. the wishlist extension for Woocommerce. 

Getting started with an open-source platform is free, but you may have to pay for premium extensions to get added functionality.

2. Hosted (SaaS)

For those looking for a ready out-of-box solution, hosted ecommerce platforms provide both hosting and the functionality of an ecommerce website. Thanks to Saas technology, the software you need to run an ecommerce site is uploaded online and is available for a monthly fee. 

With SaaS type platforms, hosting is already provided. All you need is a domain name. Some even add a free domain name to their subscription package. If you already have a domain,you can simply link your domain to your ecommerce site.

The biggest advantage of SaaS based ecommerce platforms is that they are the most beginner- friendly and you don’t need much technical support. Most service providers provide 24/ 7 support. You also don’t need to bother about updates, bug fixes etc because they handle all that.

Aside from responsive tech support, most hosted platforms make it easy to scale your business fast. They offer custom plans depending on your business’ needs. 

3. Mixed (CaaS)

Unlike the 1st two options, CaaS or  Commerce-as-a-Service platforms provide ecommerce functionality as a standalone service. No need to worry about hosting or migrating your website’s content just get ecommerce functionality.

It lets you move your e-commerce data and computing into the cloud, making it dynamic and easy-to-scale whilst giving you flexibility with the front-end of your choice. Because CaaS provides mainly cloud-based ecommerce functionality, it’s very cost effective.

It also provides easy scalability, reduces the risk of security threats and malicious attacks. You won’t have to worry about PCI and GDPR compliance because your service provider will handle that for you. 

Remember, the key is to always have your business needs in mind when picking an ecommerce platform. 

Tips and Strategies for Using eCommerce Platforms Effectively 

Ecommerce offers numerous benefits compared to traditional retail. From improving customer experience to making your products/services globally accessible, ecommerce platforms will help you expand and grow your business in new ways.

Much as ecommerce is expanding, it’s not enough to just create a web store and leave it at that. Between 12-24 million ecommerce stores currently exist, with more being added daily. Your store needs to be unique and offer a stellar shopping experience to stand out from the crowd.

To fully harness the potential of ecommerce platforms, there are few strategies you need to follow.

1. Your store should stand out visually.

The online space is crowded with lots of content all jostling for your buyer’s attention. One of the best ways to do that, is to create a visually appealing website.  Studies claim that it takes 0.05 seconds for a user to decide whether or not they like your website. 

That means you have literally a split second to create a good first impression. Your website’s colors, logo and branding should build trust and pique your visitor’s interest. It should also make it easy for them to understand what your business is about.

Visitors to your websites should have a seamless and intuitive experience. Navigating your website and finding what they are looking for should be easy for potential buyers.

A good place to start is to pick an appropriate theme or template. Make sure it fits your niche and would appeal to your ideal buyer. Be sure to add appropriate calls-to-action that would pull your visitor deeper into your website, for example, a discount or promo code on checkout.

2. Your website should be mobile-ready.

Statistics show that over 63% of internet users used mobile to browse online compared to a computer. 

It’s a fact that more and more web users log on to the internet via their mobile phone tablet. Thus, it is critical for your ecommerce site to look as good on mobile devices as it does on PCs.

Apart from lookin good, it must also load fast and operate seamlessly on mobile. 

Your ecommerce platform should help you optimize your site for both mobile and PC. Be sure to select themes and templates optimized for mobile. All your pages should be responsive on mobile especially home, product, category and checkout pages.

Shopify and Wix both do a great job at offering mobile-ready sites and themes from the onset.

3. Make your online store easy to navigate

If you’ve ever been on a website that seemed cluttered and not well- organized, then you know how important easy navigation is.  Navigation, like page load speed, influences how long visitors spend on your page.

A well-organized mobile website improves both the user experience and conversion rate on your site. To make things easier for potential customers, your ecommerce platform will have plugins that can boost navigation.

To further improve how mobile-friendly your  website is, you can download plugins and extensions that improve all user experience and may even boost engagement and sales. 

For instance, WooCommerce allows you to automatically add a plugin that makes it easy to view your previously viewed products.

4. Offer top-notch customer service

According to Bain, a superior customer experience turns a customer into a promoter with a lifetime value of 6 to 14 times that of a detractor. With more and more companies improving the overall customer service experience, you can’t afford to have a poor customer experience. 

90% of Americans use customer service as a factor in deciding whether or not to do business with a company. (HubSpot)

Other reasons why you’ll want to invest in a great customer service include maintaining your existing customer base and also the ability to cross-sell or upsell products or services. 

Your ecommerce platform should help you respond to customers whenever and wherever. This could be via a Facebook Messenger plugin or Live Chat for times when you’re offline. It may also be a simple Whatsapp plugin that links directly to your customer care chat line.

On the other hand, if a customer’s basic customer service expectations are not met, the model shows a depreciation, leading to the customer having a bad review about the business.

There are some basic requirements that are neutral and don’t necessarily count towards customer happiness or dissatisfaction. Things like phone etiquette, being courteous or polite regardless of whichever channel the customer uses, fall in this category. 

Most ecommerce platforms do a decent job at helping you provide a stellar customer care experience . Woocommerce for example has plugins that allow you to add direct Whatsapp chats to your website. 

It goes without saying then, that your ecommerce strategy should have an efficient live chat and customer service system – preferably 24/7.

Let’s take the example of Squarespace.

It makes it easy to add customer-based tools like Pure Chat, a live chat software, in just a few simple steps.

With the live chat option, you can:

  • Respond right away to website visitors’ questions
  • Engage leads, prospective customers and build trust
  • Boost your conversion rates and sales by offering discounts and solving any issues affecting a customer’s order.

5. Tap into data and analytics

If content is king, then data is the oil that keeps the content wheel spinning. After all the hard work you put into making sure your store looks great and responds well, you need to know what your potential customers are looking for when they come to your site and how they behave. 

Most ecommerce platforms provide comprehensive data via visitor tracking, analytics and sales reports. This information helps you to fully understand your customer’s behaviour so you can strategize your sales and marketing plans.

Which products do they spend the most time on? What are their demographics and geolocations? What point in the buying process do they get to before abandoning a cart? 

Understanding your customers’ behaviour and what they respond well to helps you to customize their shopping experience and boost conversion rates. 

Wix for example, has a visitor analytics tool.

Wix’ in-built visitor data analytics tool, Visitor Analytics, helps you track the number of visitors, frequency of visitors,  their countries  and IP addresses, check conversion statistics and create data visualizations.  

This will help you take better, well-informed decisions regarding the direction of any sales or marketing campaigns for the future. You’ll be able to narrow or broaden your campaigns to include as many of your visitor demographics as possible.

Struggling to find an innovative way to personalize your customers’  experiences on your eCommerce site? Give them the ability to have a live chat with your customer service rep.  Now, that’s a valuable way to provide support that feels personal and makes your visitor feel valued.

One of your goals should be to prevent your customers from becoming frustrated between the browsing and buying stages. To hold their hand and lead them through the entire buying process.

6. Strategize for all your sales channels

Most ecommerce channels allow you to sell on multiple platforms and channels including social media and marketplaces like Amazon and eBay. Each of these channels has unique marketing tactics that are best suited to it. 

For example adding custom buy buttons to your products on Amazon or Shop Now buttons on social media may boost sales conversion rates.

Ecwid allows you to tag products in your posts and stories so your shoppers don’t have to leave the app just to buy your products. They will be automatically transported to a checkout page to complete their order with zero hassle.

Purchase Criteria: How to Choose the Right eCommerce Platform? 

We’ve seen the tips and strategies you need to know to make the most out of your ecommerce platform. You’re probably also familiar with big name ecommerce platforms by now. The question is how do you decide which of the paid platforms to use? 

There are few simple factors that should influence your choice. Ultimately, your platform should help you expand and grow your business as seamlessly as possible.

1. Mobile Optimized

Whatever ecommerce platform you choose needs to give you mobile-ready templates and themes. About 40% of online transactions are done using a mobile device and nearly half of mobile users switch to your competitor after a bad experience with your mobile site. 

With almost everyone accessing the web from their phone, it is imperative that your ecommerce service provider helps you create mobile-friendly websites. This includes responsive homepages, product pages, categories and a smooth checkout process. 

2. Ease of Use

Time is money. The last thing you want is a platform with a steep learning curve that makes it difficult for you and your team to use. 

Your ecommerce platform needs to be intuitive and user-friendly with a minimal onboarding time, especially if you are not tech-inclined or have dedicated tech support. 

On the other hand, if your business is rapidly growing and you need more customization or perhaps have hired someone with IT knowledge, you’re better off with a more advanced platform like Magento or PinnacleCart that suits these needs. 

3. Scalability

Some platforms are better suited for scalability than others. Knowing things like your expected transaction volume, monthly order volume and seasonal upswings will help you pick a service provider that can accommodate all your needs as your business grows.

Some ecommerce plans limit the number of products you can add to your store, the number of staff accounts you can have per subscription, the total transaction volume allowed annually and even the number of orders monthly. 

All these factors influence the rate at which your business grows and expands. PinnacleCart and 3dcart both offer plans with unlimited products and support large management teams.

4. Extensions, Integrations and Apps

This is a dealbreaker for any ecommerce platform worth considering. Much as many of these web-based software try to incorporate the basic tools you need to run both the front and back-end of an online store, they simply can’t cover everything. 

It is for this reason that apps, extensions and integrations exist. These tools extend and improve the functionality of an ecommerce platform. They make it easy to sync your favorite business tools with your store to create an efficient workflow, for example, Aweber integration for email marketing. 

Not all ecommerce platforms have a well-populated app/extension store. Some of the newer platforms are still populating their stores, making them less appealing compared to their more established counterparts. 

The rule is to make sure whatever platform you choose has all the apps and integrations you need to maximize efficiency and productivity in your business. 

5. Payment Options

Last but not the least is payment options. One of the major reasons anyone starts an ecommerce site is to enable customers to pay for their products online. This is made possible by having varied payment options.

Having a variety of payment options improves customer experience by making it easy for them to pay whichever way they find convenient. It also builds trust and makes your website look more credible. 

It is crucial to have a platform that supports as many payment options as possible, especially if you want to build a global brand. Your platform should allow you to add all major payment processors to your site. 

Ecommerce Platforms FAQ 

These FAQs will help summarize our entire conversation so far on ecommerce platforms and how they can help you take your online business to the next level. 

What Is An Ecommerce Platform?

An ecommerce platform is a software that helps you to sell products and services online. It handles everything related to managing an online store or business from accepting payments, order fulfilment and tracking inventory. It allows you to have a front-end your customers can shop from and a back-end where you run operations of your business.

How much do eCommerce platforms cost?

Ecommerce platforms come at different price points. There are free, open-source options that don’t cost anything to install but come with added cost of paid plugins to maintain e. g Woocommerce. Paid options  can start from as low as $8.99/month (Zyro) to $79/month(PinnacleCart) for more advanced features.

What are the top 5 ecommerce platforms by number of paying clients?

The top ecommerce platforms based on highest number of customers are

– Shopify powers over a million stores online
– Squarespace has approximately 260,000 websites 
– Wix ecommerce stores serve 89,000+ sites
– Volusion has hosted 59,169 websites
– Magento hosts 260,000 merchants on its platform.

Can I change eCommerce platforms easily?

Yes. Most ecommerce platforms offer migration support services to help smooth the process and make it as painless as possible. However, migration may come with added fees depending on where you migrate to.

Which eCommerce platform has the lowest transaction fees?

A fair number of ecommerce platforms have transaction fees that may include a fixed price plus a percentage charged on every transaction. The good news is, some platforms don’t charge any fees at all. 3dcart, Zyro, Sellfy and PinnacleCart all give you zero transaction fees

Ecommerce Platform Comparison and Next Steps to Launch Your Online Store

Starting an ecommerce business is simple if you know the right tools and software to get started with.

There is a wide variety of ecommerce platforms to choose from regardless of your tech knowledge, budget, size of your business or business needs.

  • If you’re on a budget, try an open-source platform like Woocomerce or nopCommerce.
  • For a more beginner-friendly, one-stop shop solution that needs zero tech-skills and supports dropshipping, try Shopify or Wix Stores.
  • For large enterprise level ecommerce sites, PinnacleCart and Magento offer advanced functionality with more room for customization compared to other SaaS platforms.

The ecommerce industry is on an upward climb with no signs of slowing down. So if you’re looking to get your share of the pie, ecommerce platforms provide a simple way to tap into and benefit from this industry.
