User Engagement Statistics & Trends

After spending all that time creating your content, it’s obvious you’d like to know if your readers love your content or despise it. That’s where user engagement comes in. The statistics responsible for disclosing blog engagement are –

  • Page Views – An extremely basic metric that offers information to track incoming traffic to your blog.
  • Bounce Rate – A bounce occurs when a reader enters and leaves your site with no other interaction. A high bounce rate (70%+) shows Google that visitors don’t find your content appealing and hence may affect your search engine indexing.
  • New/Returning Visitors – Whether the visitors coming to your blog are first-time or returning ones. Helps to track how loyal your visitors are or how many new visitors you’ve attracted in a period.
  • Time Spent on Page – The total time the visitor spent on a single blog page. If you’ve written a 2000+ blog and a user spends only 10 seconds, you can be sure the content isn’t interesting.
  • Average Session Duration – The average time spent by visitors on your entire blog. The more time spent signifies your blog has interesting content.
  • Unique Visitors – Visitors that enter your blog at least once during a reporting period. 10 unique visitors may mean 10 new users individually accessing your blog. The higher the count the better your user engagement.
  • Scroll/Page Depth – Stat that measures how deep your audience scrolls into the article before they stop reading. A high rating indicates that the content is easy to read and is interesting enough to continue reading.
  • Comments – The number of comments left by your visitors’ showcases they’ve interacted with your blog and are leaving behind their thoughts on it.
  • Shares – If a visitor loves your content, they’ll share your blog through popular social media apps for others to enjoy.

All of these metrics can be accessed via Google Analytics, Google Search Console, WordPress Dashboard, WordPress plugins, and other UX tracking software.

User engagement is a core part of content creation. After all, you write blogs for your readers to find value and stay informed.

In the earlier monetization statistic, we revealed that only 19% of bloggers make money. This leaves 81% making less than $100 over their lifetime. Quite discouraging!

But instead of being troubled by the 81%, we choose to follow the path of the 19%. We asked questions like:

– What do successful bloggers do to improve their earnings?
– What is the information being shared by the elite bloggers?
– How do we restructure our blogs to mirror their success?

And like a light at the end of the tunnel, we came across a staggering reality.

Of, course! How did we miss it?

The high-income bloggers are all:

– Answering questions for free instead of simply asking people to buy their 4-figure course that teaches them to earn X amount in Y days
– Providing value-added research instead of weaving fairy tales out of the blue
– Dispensing facts and a roadmap instead of inventing tall tales and myths

In short, the 19% are successful because they won the loyalty of their readers which were lost on the other 81%.

That’s why, as painfully hard as it was to collect all these statistics, we made it our life’s goal to provide our readers with the industry’s best-kept secrets.

In the end, we were left with two options.

Win the trust of our readers or make money selling this info. The answer came easy.

Is blogging dead in 2020 or what’s the future of blogging?

Conventional blogging is surely dead in 2020. In fact, it died a couple of years back.

It’s bad news for people that blogged using shortcuts and ineffective data. Yet, for people ready to put in the efforts and offer a quality read. The future of blogging sure looks bright and is here to stay.

If you noticed an earlier stat in the general section, the average time to create a blog post is increasing every year. That’s because with every passing year, creating a high-quality blog takes time and effort.

A single blog post can contain:

– A survey backlink to the research conducted
– An infographic to provide awareness to its users
– A video link to offer in-depth knowledge
– Images to help prove a point
– Several visual elements to help the reader consume the info easily
– Plugins to help keep the information structured and load instantly
– Investment in a solid web host to maintain a 24/7/ 365 days uptime

The future of blogging is a highly competitive one. The survival of the fittest. With every top blogger updating their outdated info to keep relevant data.

If you believe you have what it takes to get the above tasks done. You’ve carved yourself a nice slice of the good life while offering engaging content to your readers.

Why are blogs effective? 

When blogs first began, they were a way for people to share their thoughts and ideas. An online journal.

Today, blogging is the livelihood of many professionals. Blogging is an extremely cost-effective way to educate your audience about products and acquire new customers.

Some benefits of blogging include:

– An effective source of an organic audience that is sourced directly from search engines
– Helps explain your products and redirects customers to make a purchase instantly
– Defines the goals and vision of a company as well as the brand message
– Easily shareable on social media platforms, an effective strategy to build referral traffic
– Excellent lead generation when an effective CTA is added
– A long-term commitment helps improves results even on a decade-old post

Let’s put it this way, previously we discussed how businesses that blogged gained an increase in 55% more visitors. Are you willing to lose that many site visitors and a host of other benefits to your competitors with blogs?

We didn’t think so.

Why waste time reinventing your blog strategies? When a successful game plan has already been laid out for you. Simply, copy the winning formula and be on your way to launching your own high-income blog.

  • Are your visitors leaving upon landing on your blog? Your web design is probably to blame.
  • Not enough organic traffic? Build your domain authority through guest posts and SEO.
  • Do your customers refuse to buy your products? Write a high-quality blog post to review the product and educate them.
  • Waiting for Google to index your latest blog post? 10 weeks is the average wait time.
