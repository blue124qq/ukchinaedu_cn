15 Best Website Monitoring Services and Tools for 2020

Updated on November 20, 2020
Written by Martin Luenendonk
Written by Martin Luenendonk

Every single website is built to achieve a specific purpose whether its an e-commerce platform, a blog, or a company website. However, how do you know if yours has been achieving what it was built for?

Do you think that installing a few analytics plugins for your website is what you need to track how your website has been performing? Heres what an analytics report would look like.

Website performance is quite different from just understanding where your website visitors come from and how many of them buy from you. 

A good performing website is one that has the least downtime, loads quickly, is accessible all around the globe, and is fully functional.

Let us look at the performance report of a website.

Now, how do you know if your website has been performing on these parameters?

Of course, you could ask your friends and family in different parts of the world to go through your website on different devices and tell you if your website is working. But, would that qualify as a decent sample size? Would their definition of its working perfectly fine be apt? 

How do you know if your website is compatible with every device that exists? 

It might sound like a herculean task and you might feel as though having a website now seems like more of an effort than what its worth.

But, before you jump to this conclusion, allow us to introduce you to web monitoring services and how they can be the website watchdog you never thought you could have! 

With website monitoring services, you can be assured that if anything on your website malfunctions, even if its in the middle of the night, you will be informed and you can look into it before any of your users notice.

Lets begin.

1. StatusCake

Best all in one website monitoring services solution.

StatusCake is one of the easiest to use website monitoring services that can help you generate powerful reports about your websites health. 

Giants like Google, Netflix, NASA, and a few others use their services! Not convinced? Allow us to tell you why StatusCake could be your monitoring suite. 

While most websites work well with HTTP monitoring, StatusCake provides a wide range of advanced options for you to choose from based on your plan. The various tests that StatusCake can perform for you include TCP, SMTP, SSH, PING along with the regular HTTP tests.

StatusCake allows you to check the speed at which your page loads and tracks the server response time, load time calculated in milliseconds, the size of the page, and the number of requests that are needed for the page to load. 

Not only can you choose how often the speed tests are conducted (i.e. every 1, 5, 10, 30 minutes) but you will also be intimated if any particular page takes longer than usual to load. 

It also alerts you as soon as your SSL certificate loses validity or is due for renewal.

StatusCakes domain monitoring feature can help you identify if your website has been flagged or blacklisted or marked as spam. You also get details about your domain and alerts you when you have an upcoming plan renewal.

Along with monitoring the health of your server, StatusCake allows you to monitor your CPU, RAM, or storage helping you monitor your resource utilization. 

StatusCake is one of the easiest tools, especially for beginners. You can always take the help of their tutorials for beginners and they also have a vast knowledge base for developers that can help you become a pro at effectively managing your website. 

You can generate and share customized reports about your websites health with clients and employees to highlight specific bits of information. 

While most website monitoring tools send alerts through SMS and calls, StatusCake can be integrated into 18 different platforms such as Twitter, Telegram, DataDog, Discord, Microsoft Teams, Slack, and others.

Pricing

There are three plans for you to choose from. 

You can start with the Free plan and use it as a trial. Once you feel that your website has been performing efficiently because of the service, you can switch to the other plans. What is Website Monitoring?

Imagine the person you assigned to monitor your website takes sick leave one day. 

The last he checked the websites performance was the previous day at noon. You get an email from a potential customer who says that he has been trying to access your website for the past 24 hours.

He wants to know whether you are still in business!

Not great for your business, is it?

How many customers did you lose during these 24 hours? Has it been more than 24 hours that your website was down? What if you had received notification as soon as your website went down? 

Here is where website monitoring comes in. 

There are two different types of website monitoring, real user monitoring, and synthetic user monitoring.

  • Real user monitoring allows you to study the behavior and experience of real users who visit your website.
  • Synthetic User Monitoring simulates user journeys on your website to help you identify broken links on your pages like a broken link before a user checkouts in the shopping cart.

Unlike manual monitoring, website monitoring happens round the clock. 

The service that you choose helps you analyze the performance, availability, and functioning of your website. Once your service detects an anomaly on your page, most website monitoring tools also offer topology maps that help you narrow down to the root cause. 

You can use it to monitor just ONE of your pages or your ENTIRE website. 

It helps you measure the uptime of your website and also ensures that your website is available to everyone all around the globe. The best website monitoring services monitor your websites from multiple testing nodes across different locations like SmartBear Alertsite has 350 testing nodes. 

Irrespective of where you are in the world, you can still use website monitoring to keep a check on your sites performance. 

You can create multi-member alert groups and integrate the tool with tools like JIRA and Slack so that your entire team gets alerts and everyone stays in the loop.

Website monitoring helps you detect any unauthorized changes that might have happened or can help you identify threats to your site and the data shared by your users. 

These services also help you ensure that the SSL certificate of your website is valid and alert you close to the renewal. You can also get details about your domain, updates about renewal, and determine if your domain has been marked as spam or has been blacklisted.

Now there are two levels on which websites get monitored.

  • Traditional local monitoring, the focus here is more on the servers health rather than user experience on your site. If the server works, it confirms that the website is also working.
  • Global monitoring, on the other hand, studies the server, monitors the uptime, checks for accessibility across the globe, and identifies DNS issues if there are any.

The best website monitoring tools are agentless and once you install them, all you have to do is solve the root cause of any malfunction on your page that the tool identifies for you. 

Benefits of Using Website Monitoring Tools

Your website must always be up and running.

It must be accessible to everyone around the globe, even if a user searches from the tiniest and most remote village where your business might never expand. 

While the internal infrastructure like the website design, the codes, scripts, forms determine the way your website performs, the external factors like the load time, response time, website availability determine the user experience. 

All of these make it imperative to monitor your website 24/7. 

Let us look at how website monitoring tools and services can help you seamlessly manage your website and its performance.

1. Never face an error message again with 24/7 performance monitoring

Website monitoring tools always send you alerts whenever they detect a glitch on your page. Through synthetic monitoring, you can even detect problems even before they happen.

There is hardly an instance where your website will be down and you will not know because performance monitoring tools send alerts through different channels like SMS, calls, emails, and other services like Microsoft Teams, Slack, and others. 

You are in control of when you want to be alerted, who must be alerted, how to raise escalations if an alert goes unnoticed and status alerts after an issue gets resolved.

2. Get detailed information about your servers health through infrastructure monitoring

The best monitoring tools allow you to monitor your internal infrastructure through their offerings where you can keep track of the world behind your firewall. 

Whether its your server that is causing your website to load slower, the number of HTTP calls required when a request is made you can monitor your servers health as well.

Keep track of resource utilization, CPU, storage monitoring, or determining whether your current bandwidth is sufficient to get the desired page load time, website monitoring services give you detailed analytics reports mostly from one customizable dashboard.

3. Receive immediate alerts about website downtime and identify the root cause

Even though website owners, especially, e-commerce platforms dread downtime, sometimes it may be inevitable. Your website could be down due to some programming error on your end, or it could be down if your server itself is down. 

Most website monitoring tools alert you within seconds of your website going down and even notify you about micro-downtimes. They also allow you to schedule maintenance windows so that you dont get false alerts when your site is down for maintenance.

Heres an example of how a website monitoring service tells you precisely about the downtime of your website. 

If your website has been facing downtime quite often recently, your website monitoring service might be able to help you spot a trend and identify the main culprit. 

If its your programming that has been causing the error, you can have your developers look at it immediately.

If it is the server, then probably it is time to look at other options. 

4. 24/7 agentless website monitoring

Unlike manually monitoring your website, website monitoring services work round the clock throughout the year. 

Agentless website monitoring services take care of all your website monitoring needs automatically and even deliver reports in PDF and other formats to your mailbox. 

If you opt for an agent-based website monitoring service, you might still have to deploy one person to oversee that the monitoring tool has been functioning properly.

You can choose how often you want your website to be monitored, i.e., every 30 seconds, 1 minute, 5 minutes, 10 or 30 minutes, or just once or twice a day. 

5. Narrow down to the root cause of your websites malfunction

Apart from just telling you about the websites uptime and downtime, website monitoring services also give you a detailed report about the specific elements on your page that have been creating any trouble. 

Whether its the forms, the scripts, the URL a website monitoring report gives you a detailed analysis. 

At the first look of it, some services might give you an overview in big numbers, or a green smiley face or a red frown (like Dynatrace UEM does) indicating whether your website is in good health or needs attention. 

When you scroll down further, you get an in-depth report of what has been going wrong, country-wise, and since when. Here is an example. 

6. Easy installation and detailed tutorials

No one would want their potential customers to turn to their competitors just because their solution is too difficult to use, would they?

Website monitoring tools are therefore almost always very easy to install and set up. You might have to simply install a plugin or download an agent or a collector for your website. For some, all you have to do is log in to their portal and enter your URL. 

Once you have set up your tool, your tool will start studying the websites infrastructure, environment, and performance and start sending you notifications and reports. 

Most of these services have step-by-step instructions or video tutorials and blogs making the entire process extremely easy.

Montastic and UptimeRobot have very easy-to-use interfaces and services which is why they probably do not require any installation guides or tutorials.

7. Monitor real user experiences on your site

When your users visit your website, there is a Javascript snippet that is placed in the <head> tag of the HTML code of the pages that collect information about your websites performance from the users of your page.

Using web monitoring tools will help you monitor how your users have been navigating on your page and give you insights into the users journey, helping you identify the scope of improvement if any. 

RUM also gives you insights into how many people visited your page, country-wise load time, the number of active sessions, bounce rate, devices used, browsers used, and the total number of visits. 

If your most valuable customers come from a particular location or use a particular device, these insights could help you optimize your pages accordingly.

Some web monitoring services might also allow you to isolate each of your user experiences and study them in detail using their session tracing feature like the one that New Relic offers. 

This would give you a clearer view of what went wrong for a particular user, at what time, in which country, on which device, which browser was used, and also how long their session on your page lasted. 

8. Detect issues on your website even before they happen by creating simulations

Create custom scripts and simulate user journeys on your page using the website monitoring tools before your visitors come to your site. 

You can use this feature along with the transaction monitoring feature to ensure that there is no broken link on your website and all elements that are dependent on each other work fine.

AppDynamics provides its users with a conceptual map that shows all the dependencies on your page and helps you trace your way back to the root cause of a problem.

9. Can be scaled as per business needs

Most services allow you the flexibility to scale as your business grows. If you initially opted for a personal plan, you can always choose an enterprise plan and scale up your monitoring solution. 

When your business site has sensitive customer data that could be of interest to other parties, your business also runs the risk of getting attacked by hackers. 

In 2010, Amazon users in the UK were unable to access the website during Christmas for half an hour due to some hardware failure or as rumors had it because it was under threat. 

Website monitoring services help you narrow down on such possibilities by spotting timeout errors, security loopholes, server overload, CPU bottleneck, and lag or latency. 

10. Generate custom reports for yourself and all your stakeholders within seconds

While some website monitoring services like DataDog, require you to create your reports manually, others generate reports for you automatically. 

You can choose between daily, monthly, and weekly reports or generate them instantly within 60 seconds. You may also be allowed to use filters and include only those parameters that matter to you and your business the most. 

Additionally, website monitoring services allow you to create public reports and place them on public status pages that can be viewed by your colleagues and customers. The purpose of these reports is to show that your website meets all the required SLAs and is a safe site to visit.

You can choose to do all of these manually but, we all know that humans err.

With multiple factors to monitor every single minute of the day, your moderators will likely miss a few of them at least once a day. 

Each minute your website does not work as expected, you run the risk of losing out to your competitors who might be the next best alternative! If it does not load in 3 seconds, 6-39% of users will hit the block button.

Website monitoring services make the entire process extremely easy and manageable.

Another plus is that you would probably have to pay a much lesser price for a website monitoring service compared to what you pay to your moderators and the cost of a missed opportunity. 

How to Choose the Best Website Monitoring Tool? 

As human beings, businesses are separate entities that have their very own specific needs. 

If you are an e-commerce platform, your goal would be to drive traffic to your website and get your visitors to make purchases from you. If you are an architect, the aim of your website would be to generate qualified leads. 

Since each business is different, a solution that fits your business might not fit mine. The same principle applies when it comes to choosing website monitoring tools. 

While choosing a website monitoring tool it is essential to match the capabilities, features, price, and reports generated to ensure that they are relevant to your business and serve your purpose. 

A good website monitoring tool must be such that it allows you to customize features based on your requirement, has browser monitoring, real user monitoring, synthetic monitoring, multiple testing locations, frequent alerts, and much more. 

Let us look at each of the most important components that you must consider before choosing a website monitoring tool.

1. Pricing

Every business works on a budget which is why it is important to choose one that not only fits your budget but also addresses what you are looking for. Higher prices do not automatically indicate better services. 

While some services might also be available for free, most service providers follow a freemium model where certain basic features might be available for free and the premium services will be available to only those who subscribe. 

You should always opt for one that allows you to cancel the subscription at any time and also offers a substantial trial period.

2. Ease of use

Irrespective of who is responsible for overseeing whether the website monitoring service has been performing as expected, the best website monitoring tool must be easy to use. 

Whether its you, your developer, your software team, or a new joiner, the service you choose must be easy to configure and the reports must be comprehensible at every level.

Most website monitoring tools have clear instructions on how to get the most of their service.

3. Number of testing locations

When you are serving a global audience, it becomes very important that your local audience as well as your global audience have the exact same kind of experience. 

For this to happen, it is imperative to have testing nodes across the world.

While choosing a solution, the higher the number of testing locations, the better the coverage. Higher testing locations indicate that the website performance will be monitored in all those locations as well.

4. Multiple browser support

You need to choose a service that allows you to study the users behavior and experience on your site. Your users might not only come from different locations and use different devices, but they may also use different browsers.

Choose a website monitoring tool that allows you to monitor real-time users across different browsers and not just the most common one, i.e., Google Chrome.

5. Business-specific features or scope for customization

While uptime monitoring might be important for one business, transaction monitoring might be more important to the other. Monitoring tools that allow you to choose the parameters most important to you must be your preferred choice. 

If you want to monitor your uptime and transactions every 30 seconds but want to run infrastructure monitoring tests just once, your website monitoring tool must allow you to do that.

6. Higher URL monitoring frequency

You may have a website, a domain, the best online shop in the world but none of it is useful if your customers cannot reach your website. Also, if your URL gets attacked, your customers data could be at risk.

The best URL monitoring services should have an integrated security feature that ensures your URL is monitored for XSS attacks, SQL injection, proper authentication and session management, storage of passwords, and more. 

Good monitoring tools also help you get the SSL certificate for your websites and send you updates close to renewal.

7. Flexibility to make changes to your plan

As your business grows, you will need to scale your website and the website monitoring services as well. Choose a service that offers enterprise plans that allow you to scale the scope of the services. 

Also, it is recommended to choose the ones that offer monthly billing plans or the option to cancel the subscription at any point in time.

8. Offers Real User Monitoring and Synthetic Monitoring

You cannot shoot in the dark. Your website cannot function on the assumption that everything works well. RUM and synthetic monitoring give you results based on real experiences and simulations run on your page. 

While real user monitoring helps you enhance the customer experience after studying the user experience, synthetic monitoring will help you detect problems even before they occur. 

Always choose a service that offers both. These could be bundled together or available individually but are both important especially for e-commerce websites. 

Website Monitoring Services FAQ

How do I monitor a website?

All you have to do is sign up for a website monitoring tool that best suits the nature of your website. There are different complex to easy tools to help you monitor your website.

All you have to do is sign up for a service, install the service or simply log in to their portal and enter your URL and the monitoring service starts monitoring your website.

Whenever the tool detects an anomaly on your page you are alerted on the channel that you choose for the alerts. Your team can then look into it and resolve it immediately.

How do I check the downtime of a website?

Once you have registered with or installed a performance monitoring service provider, most often you will receive an alert whenever your website is down. Some services even send you alerts about micro-downtimes.

However, you can always head to the dashboard and check the status of your website. Generally, your website uptime is shown in green, and downtime is shown in red.

Whats the economical damage of my website being down?

Economical damage caused by your website being down is massive especially if you are an e-commerce platform. Imagine your page being down on the day of a big sale that could have earned you millions in revenue.

While you do not know when and why your website could go down at any point in time, you can always calculate the risk and be prepared with a contingency plan.

Look at some historical data and determine the probability of downtime. You can then use the formula given below to calculate the risk.

Risk = Probability of Downtime x Potential Impact of Downtime

You can calculate the impact based on the clients you lose per minute that your website is down.
