Step 1: Pick A Profitable Niche You Are Passionate About

The first step to building a blogging business is to find a blogging niche that is both profitable and that you are passionate about.

To find the right niche for your blog, start by identifying your skills and passions. Being skilled in your niche allows you to provide valuable content that will solve your audiences problem, while being passionate about your niche gives you the motivation to blog consistently, even before the money starts flowing in.

To identify your skills and passions, consider the things you are naturally good at, some of the goals you have achieved in life, your academic qualifications, and your professional experience.

Once you have identified your skills and passions, the next step is to identify the right audience. Remember, you are not starting a blog for yourself, but for your audience. Therefore, you need to identify the problem that is driving your target audience to read your blog.

Here, you also need to determine whether you are targeting individual customers or businesses, whether your target audience has a budget for what you are offering, and whether they are actually willing to spend money on what you are offering.

The third step is to identify the result your target audience is looking for. What do they want to achieve after using the solution you are offering? Identifying the result your audience is looking for allows you to create the best solution and come up with a unique selling proposition.

Finally, you need to determine the audience revenue potential of your chosen niche. Audience revenue potential is determined by two things: amount of traffic within the niche, and the amount of revenue per visitor. You should aim for niches with high traffic and high revenue per visitor.

Step 3: Write Helpful Evergreen Blog Posts Using Keyword Research

Content is the most important element on your blog. It doesnt matter how well designed your blog is, or how aggressively you market your blog; if you do not have great content on your blog, your visitors will not stick around for long enough to allow you to sell anything.

So, how do you create great content for your blog?

The key to high quality content that attracts and captivates readers is to create helpful, evergreen content. Evergreen content refers to content that is optimized for search engines like Google, and content that remains valuable for a long period of time. In other words, evergreen content is content that will be relevant several years from the day you publish it.

For instance, lets assume you create content on How To Make Money During A Pandemic. At the moment, since the world is going through a pandemic, such content will attract a lot of traffic, since it is something people are actually concerned about. However, two or three years down the line, after the pandemic has ended, no one will care about making money during a pandemic, and your traffic from this blog post will dry up.

On the other hand, if you created an article on 10+ Legitimate Ways To Make Money On The Side, such an article will continue driving traffic to your blog 10 years down the line, because people will always be interested in learning how they can make more money.

The beauty of evergreen content is that it continues driving more and more traffic as time passes (according to blogging statistics), compared to regular content which attracts less and less traffic as time passes.

To create helpful, evergreen content for your blog, start by brainstorming some ideas that have evergreen potential. Here are a few examples of blog post ideas with evergreen potential.

  • Frequently asked questions about your industry or your products and services.
  • How to guides and tutorials
  • Articles explaining complex industry concepts
  • Insider information about your industry

Once you come up with potential ideas, the next thing you need to do is to perform keyword research to help you identify the right keywords. Remember, if people are not searching for that topic, your content will not be of much use.

When it comes to performing keyword research for your evergreen content, we recommend using SEMrush. SEMrush is one of the best keyword research and analytics tools available on the internet.

For instance, if you are in the car insurance niche and your search for the term car insurance, SEMrush provides you with a list of some of the common keywords and related questions that people are searching for and their search numbers. This can help you figure out which keywords you need to focus on when creating your content.

Once you have identified the best keywords to target, the next step is to create helpful content around these keywords. By helpful content, I am talking about content that:

  • Helps your readers solve their problem
  • Is written in easy, simple language that can be understood by beginners
  • Is properly formatted to make it easy to read
  • Includes examples and visual aids to make it easier for readers to understand
  • Speaks naturally and directly to the reader
  • Doesnt use jargon or overly technical language

Once you are done writing your blog post, make sure that it is optimized for search engines by

  • Ensuring the keywords are used naturally throughout the blog post
  • Creating an SEO friendly URL for the blog post with the keyword included
  • Using your keywords on the page title, title tags, subheadings, image alt tags, and meta description
  • Adding links to authoritative sites
  • Adding hyperlinks to other evergreen content on your blog

Finally, have a plan to update your evergreen blog posts regularly to ensure that they remain relevant. If you fail to update your blog posts, they might become slightly outdated and lose their evergreen value.

2. Offer Consulting Services To Businesses (Or Consumers)

Everyone probably knows a pompous consultant who operates from a posh office, wears bespoke Italian suits, and drives around in a fancy car. This kind of consultant gives off the perception that consulting is something that is reserved for the chosen few.

What you might not know is that you can also start making money on the side by offering consulting services to businesses or individual consumers.

The best part is that you do not even need to get any special certifications to become a consultant. You have everything you need to start offering consulting services.

Consulting is engaging in the business of giving expert advice to people working in a specific field. The Oxford Dictionary

From the Oxford Dictionary definition, consulting requires two key things: expert advice and a specific field. Therefore, if you are skilled enough in a specific field, you have everything you need to offer consulting services.

Why consulting though? Is it such a lucrative industry?

According to Statista, the size of the global consulting industry has been growing steadily over the last 10 years and is set to hit close to $300 billion by the end of 2020.

Of course, you might be thinking that you dont stand a chance considering that there are already established firms in this industry, such as the MBB firms. 

However, research shows that one fifth of all consulting work is performed by independent consultants, rather than big consultancy firms. Wouldnt you want to get your share of the $6 billion that goes to independent consultants?

To start a side business offering consulting services, you need to do two things:

  • Craft your consulting offer
  • Focus on one paid marketing channel to acquire customers

Craft Your Consulting Offer

Before you can start a consulting business, you need to get clear on your consulting offer. What advice are you offering? To whom? How will it help them?

Without a clear, well-structured consulting offer, you will have a hard time attracting clients or delivering the right results.

To come up with a clear, well-structured offer, you need to:

Determine Your Skills And Strengths

We have already seen that consulting involves offering expert advice within a specific field. If you are not an expert within your field, you cannot solve your clients problems, and no one will take your advice.

To determine your skills and strength, take a look at the following four things:

  • Things that you are naturally good at, things that people routinely come to you for help with.
  • Your academic qualifications.
  • Your professional experience.
  • The personal goals you have achieved in life.

A deep evaluation of these four things will allow you to identify things that you can do better than the average person.

For instance, if you are naturally good at computers and technology, you can offer IT consulting services.

Similarly, if you have academic qualifications in public relations, and you have previously worked as a PR executive, you can offer PR consulting services.

Identify Your Target Market

The next step of crafting your consulting offer is to identify the market segment you are targeting. Do you intend to offer your services to individual consumers or business clients? If business clients, will you focus on large corporations or SMEs?

Identifying your target market serves two purposes. First, it allows you to come up with a consulting offer that is best suited for your client. For instance, a consulting offer that works for an SME or a non-profit organization might not work for a large corporation.

Second, knowing the kind of client you are targeting allows you to determine if they have the budget and the willingness to pay for your services. 

For instance, if you are offering IT consultancy services to individual consumers, many of your potential clients might have a challenge with computers and technology, but the majority of them might not have the budget for it.

Determine What Your Market Needs

Once you have identified your skills and strengths and your target market, it is now time to figure out your target markets greatest challenges within your area of expertise.

Remember, if your target audience doesnt have a problem, then they will not pay for your advice.

An easy way to identify the biggest pain points for your target market within your area of expertise is to search for online forums related to your area of expertise. What questions are most people asking on these forums? In which topics does there seem to be a lot of confusion? These will provide you with insights into the kind of challenges your target audience are struggling with.

Alternatively, you can ask your target audience what their greatest pain points are by running an online survey.

Armed with this information, you can now craft a consulting offer that helps a specific market segment achieve a specific result within a specific field or niche. This is the holy grail of great consulting offers.

Actually, you can craft your consulting offer using this formula:

I am a [your niche] consultant who helps [your target audience] to achieve [your results] through [your offer].

Focus On One Paid Marketing Channel To Acquire Customers

Now that your consulting offer is ready, the next thing you need to do is to come up with a system for attracting clients repeatedly.

One of the most effective ways of attracting and acquiring clients for your consulting business is to create a marketing funnel comprising of the following four steps:

Choose A Paid Marketing Channel To Focus On

While it is possible to drive traffic to your consulting offer organically, the best approach for generating leads quickly is to use paid marketing channels. We recommend using paid marketing channels for the following reasons:

  • Paid marketing can be targeted based on demographics, geographic, and psychographic information.
  • Paid marketing channels provide significant ROI.
  • Paid marketing channels drive results much faster than organic channels.
  • You can measure the results of your marketing efforts and optimize for better performance.

To make your potential clients aware of your offer and generate leads, pick one paid marketing channel to focus on and set up a marketing campaign.

You can do this on Facebook, LinkedIn, Google AdWords, Instagram, YouTube, and so on, depending on where your target audience are more likely to be found.

For instance, to set up a paid marketing campaign on Facebook, start by targeting your ideal client by selecting their age, gender, location, interests, and even things like job title. 

For example, if your consulting offer is geared towards photographers in Florida, you can enter the terms photographer and Florida and Facebook will search for all people who meet this criteria.

Once you have selected the people you want to target, you will then need to create an ad that will be shown to these people, and then set your budget for the marketing campaign.

Create A Landing Page

After creating your ad, you need somewhere to direct people who click on your ad, which is why you need a landing page.

The landing page acts as an extension to your paid ad. The aim of the landing page is to convince the potential client to provide you with their contact information in exchange for something valuable.

For instance, sticking to the photographer example, you can promise an eBook or a video that will teach them how to attract better and high paying clients in exchange for their email address. For this to work, however, the promise on your landing page should meet the expectations of what you promised on the ad.

Here is an example of a great landing page from Lintense.

It is advisable to keep your landing page as simple as possible in order to get as many people as you can to sign up.

Design Your Value Proposition

Now that you have your potential clients contact information, the first thing you need to do is to provide the value you promised in the ad and on your landing page.

For instance, if you promised to teach your potential customers how to attract better clients, you need to fulfill this promise. You can do this either through a video, an eBook, a free email course, and so on.

At this point, you might be wondering how you are going to make money selling consulting services if you are already giving away your advice for free. The aim behind doing this is to give your potential clients something valuable to show them that you can actually solve their problems.

Once you have delivered on the promise of your ad and landing page, the next thing you need to do is to pitch your consulting offer to your potential clients.

Since you already have their contact information, you can do this by adding their email into an email funnel, inviting them for a webinar, or inviting them for a coaching call.

Once they sign up for your webinar or coaching call, this is where you are now going to deliver your pitch.

The aim of this step is to show the potential client that you have a deep understanding of the challenges they are struggling with and that you are capable of providing an effective and efficient solution to this challenge.

If you get this step right, you will be successful in converting some of your leads into paying customers.

Value Delivery

This is the final step of our marketing funnel. Now that you have converted some of your leads into paying customers, it is time for you to deliver on your consulting offer. If your consulting offer is to help freelance photographers build a five figure business, help your clients do exactly that.

Your success as a consultant is highly dependent on your ability to deliver value to your clients. If you can help your clients solve their problems effectively, they will spread word about your services and refer their friends and acquaintances to you. 

On the other hand, if you fail to deliver value to your clients, your consulting business wont take off, regardless of how much marketing you are doing.

Importance Of Focusing On A Paid Marketing Channel To Attract Customers

The beauty of this customer attraction system is that it is reliable and repeatable. Lets see how this works.

Lets assume that spending $100 on a paid marketing channel results in 100 people visiting your landing page. 

Of these 100, only 50 actually share their contact information with you.

Out of these 50, lets assume that only 10 end up signing up for your webinar or coaching call.

Out of these, only 2 end up paying for your consulting services at $500 each. 

This means that for every $100 you invest in marketing, you are going to make $900 in profit.

This means that any time you need to make $900 in profit, you only need to invest $100 in marketing, and you can do this as many times as possible. 

If you want to double your profit, you just need to double your marketing spend and your profits will double automatically. Following this simple system will keep customers flowing to your consulting business.

Today, millions of people work remotely for companies from the other side of the globe on a freelance basis. If you are looking for a side hustle or a way to make money from home, becoming a high paid remote freelancer could be one of your best options.

Over the last decade, the freelance economy has grown massively. As of 2019, the income earned by freelancers within the United States was close to $1 trillion according to Upwork, making up about 5% of the US GDP.

Actually, freelancing contributes more to the US economy than established industries such as mining, transportation, and construction.

If you are worried that freelancing is a low-paying option that is only reserved for those who cannot find better employment elsewhere, you need to think again. 

The study by Upwork reports that skilled freelancers earn a median rate of $28 per hour. This is higher than what 70% of workers in the US earn. 

62% of freelancers who left traditional jobs to focus on freelancing also say that they earn more today than they did in their traditional jobs.

These statistics show that there is a lot of money to be made in freelancing. The big question is, how do you become a highly paid remote freelancer?

To become a highly paid freelancer, you need to do two things.

  1. Define your freelance offering
  2. Find a good freelancing platform to help you earn money online quickly

Define Your Freelance Offering

The first step to becoming a high paid remote freelancer is to define your freelance offering. What service are you going to sell to your clients?

If you look at some of the highest paid freelancers, you will notice that they dont try to do everything. Instead, they focus on one or two core skills. Doing this allows them to position themselves as experts in that one thing and charge top dollar for their skills.

For instance, if you take a look at the Upwork profile of Danny Margulies, one of the highest paid freelance writers, you will notice that he focuses on one thing email copywriting. 

Not article writing, eBook ghostwriting, case study writing, email copywriting, landing page copywriting, and every other kind of writing there is on earth. Just email copywriting. This is why he is able to charge $150 per hour for his services.

Similarly, if you want to become a highly paid remote freelancer, you need to come up with a highly specific freelance offering, rather than trying to do everything within your industry.

To come up with your freelancing offer, you need to

To make money as a freelancer, you need to have a skill that is valuable enough for someone else to pay for.

Very often, most people base their freelance offering on their professional qualifications. For instance, if you are a qualified web developer, it makes sense to offer freelance web development services, since this is something you are already good at.

However, if you dont want to base your freelance services on your professional qualifications, you can still sell other valuable skills that you possess. A good way to identify these skills is to look at areas you naturally excel in, or areas where other people come to you for help with.

For instance, if you are naturally good at decorating houses, you can offer freelance interior design and dcor services. Similarly, if you are naturally good at writing, you can offer freelance writing services.

Identify What You Love To Do

What is the point of freelancing if you dont get to do something that you love? After identifying the valuable skills that you possess, ask yourself which of these things do I enjoy doing?

As a freelancer, you dont have a boss looking over your shoulder, and therefore, you are more likely to give it your all if you are doing something you love.

Determine If There Is A Market For Your Skill

Once you have identified your valuable skill that you enjoy, the final thing to do is to determine if you can make money selling this skill. A good way to do that is to use keyword research tools such as SEMrush to determine the number of people searching for the kind of services you intend to offer.

For example, lets say that you are a skilled graphic designer who enjoys creating infographics, and you are thinking about offering freelance infographic design services. Entering the keywords infographic design on SEMrush will tell you the number of people interested in infographic design.

Once you identify something that is at the intersection of these three factors something you are skilled at, something you enjoy doing, and something that is lucrative, you have a winning idea to base your freelance offering on.

The idea behind niche sites is quite simple build a site around a certain niche, rank for important keywords related to that niche, get lots of traffic from search engines, and then monetize the traffic through affiliate marketing and the sale of digital products.

Getting your site to dominate a niche takes quite some effort, but is it really worth the hard work? Can you make good money through a niche site? With the right approach, you can go from $0 to $1,000+ dollars within a couple of months.

To make money from a niche site, you first need to find the right niche, and then launch your affiliate blog in this niche.

For your eBook to make money, you eBook idea needs to meet three key conditions:

  • There should be a pre-existing market for the idea.
  • There should be people willing to pay for the idea.
  • The competition for the idea should be reasonable.

To determine whether your eBook idea meets these three conditions, you should ask yourself the following three questions:

Are There People Searching For My Idea?

If people are searching for books related to your idea, this shows that there is an existing market for the book you want to write.

To determine whether people are searching for books related to your idea is to perform a keyword research using a tool like SEMrush. This will give you the number of people searching for keywords related to your idea every month.

Are There People Willing To Pay For The Idea?

Sometimes, people might be searching for information but might not be willing to pay for it.

To find out whether other authors are making money, go to Amazon Kindle Store and search for keywords related to your idea to find similar books by other authors.

Click on each book that appears on the first page, scroll down to product details and note down its Amazon Best Seller Rank (ABSR).

Enter the ABSR of each of these books into an Amazon Sales Rank Calculator to find out the number of copies each book sells per day. Multiply this number by the selling price of the book and then multiply by 30 to find out how much money each author makes per month.

Using this information, you can now determine the average amount of money authors in this niche make every month. If the average monthly revenue looks good, you can move on to the third question.

You might have a good eBook idea in a profitable niche but still fail to make money because the niche or topic is too competitive.

To determine the level of competition for your book idea, go to Amazon Kindle Store and search for a keyword that best describes your book. The results page will show you the number of books related to the keyword.

For instance, if you want to publish a book on Freelance writing, typing the phrase on Amazon Kindle Store will show you how many books related to freelance writing are on Amazon.

The higher the number, the more competition you are up against.

Next, find the average ABSR number of the top three books related to your keyword. You will need to beat this ABSR in order for your book to be found organically by readers. The higher the ABSR number, the tougher the competition you are up against.

Finally, you can also check the quality of the books appearing on the first page when you search for keywords related to your topic. How are there reviews? How are the cover designs? How good are the book descriptions? The higher the quality of the books on the first page, the tougher the competition you are up against.

If you determine that there is a pre-existing market for your book, that people are buying similar books, and that the competition is not too tough, you can go ahead and write the book.

21. Become An Influencer On Instagram

If you love posting and spending time on Instagram, you are sitting on an opportunity to earn money online on Instagram doing something you love.

Influencer marketing involves building a significant following on a social media platform, and then leveraging your influence to get your followers to take specific action, such as liking a brands social media pages or buying a product.

With digital marketing becoming harder and harder every day, brands are willing to pay you handsomely if you can use your influence to positively impact their brand.

Why Instagram though?

Instagram is the best platform for influencer marketing. According to a survey by Bloglovin, 59% of micro-influencers claim that Instagram is the most effective platform for influencer marketing.

Many micro-influencers also feel that Instagram is the best platform for branded content.

In addition, marketers also agree that Instagram posts are the most effective format for influencer marketing.

So, how much can you make through Instagram influencing?

This will depend largely on the size and type of your audience. However, big influencers charge an average of $1,000 for a single Instagram post, while micro-influencers charge about $300 for a single post, on average. Some celebrities like Dwayne Johnson earn as much as $1 million for a single Instagram post.

When, How, What To Promote On Instagram To Earn Money Online

There is more to earning money as an Instagram influencer than simply having a huge following. A huge following is of no use if it doesnt drive an impact for your clients.

Here are some things to keep in mind if you want to make money as an Instagram influencer:

Focus On A Particular Niche

Instead of trying to appeal to every Instagram user, you should focus on a specific niche and create content around this niche. This will allow you to build a following of people who are interested in that niche.

With a niche, it becomes easier to partner with advertisers and brands, because you are giving them access to a targeted audience. For instance, if someone wants to market a new fashion line, it will be more effective for them to partner with a fashion influencer than with an influencer who has no specific niche.

Focus On Engagements, Rather Than Follower Count

While follower count is definitely important, follower engagement is more important. For instance, lets assume there are two influencers. Influencer A has 100k followers, while influencer B has 1 million followers.

For every post, influencer A receives over 10,000 likes and 5,000 comments, while influencer B gets just 1,000 likes and even less comments. Which of the two influencers would you prefer working with?

While influencer B has a huge follower count, the low engagement rates shows that this influencer doesnt wield much influence over his followers. Therefore, even if they publish a sponsored post, most of his followers wont engage with the post.

Influencer A, on the other hand, might have a smaller follower count, but the high engagement rates means that sponsored posts published by this influencer will drive more action compared to those of influencer B.

Therefore, if you want to be successful as an Instagram influencer, you should focus on building an engaged following, rather than simply focusing on building a huge following.

Know The Right Times To Post

The times you post your content on Instagram will have an impact on how well the content will perform. For instance, if you post content at midnight when everyone is asleep, most of your followers wont see the content.

The best times to post on Instagram for high engagement levels are at around midday and in the evening after work, with Mondays, Wednesdays and Thursdays being the best days to post. Sunday, on the other hand, is the worst day to post on Instagram.

If you follow the above pointers, you will be able to build a loyal and engaged following, and brands will start reaching out to you for partnership opportunities.

Just because you came up with what you think is a brilliant idea doesnt necessarily mean that your idea is good.

Build Your Product

Armed with customer feedback about your MVP, you can now go ahead and develop your micro SaaS software, incorporating the customer feedback into your design.

Launch And Market Your SaaS Software

Once you are done developing and beta testing your SaaS software, you can now launch your product and start marketing aggressively to attract users.
